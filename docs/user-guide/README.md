# Ankurah Community — user guide

Welcome. Ankurah Community is a live chat where every message, reaction, and
edit appears everywhere at once, the moment it happens. It is also a working
showcase of [Ankurah](https://ankurah.org), the local-first sync framework it
is built on — which is why it can literally show you its own inner workings
(see X-ray mode below).

This guide explains what you can do and how. Each page is a two-minute read.

## Contents

- [Getting started](getting-started.md) — signing in with a passkey, your
  display name, account settings, and signing out.
- [Messages](messages.md) — writing, formatting with Markdown, editing, and
  deleting.
- [Reactions](reactions.md) — the emoji reactions under each message.
- [Rooms](rooms.md) — switching rooms, creating them, unread badges, and
  topics.
- [People](people.md) — the members list, roles and badges, and profile
  cards.
- [Moderation](moderation.md) — what moderators can do, the public
  moderation log, and what a ban looks like.
- [X-ray mode](xray.md) — the showpiece: watch the live sync engine work.

## Good to know

- **It's live.** You never refresh. New messages, reactions, name changes, and
  moderator actions arrive on their own.
- **It's local-first.** Your browser keeps its own copy of the conversation and
  syncs in the background, so the app stays responsive and can even show you
  what it is doing under the hood.
- **A connection indicator** in the top bar shows whether you're currently
  connected. If it slips to *Disconnected*, the app keeps working and catches
  up automatically once it reconnects.
